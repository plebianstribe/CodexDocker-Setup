# Novelty and SWOT Framework

Use this framework to keep web verification consistent and decision-focused.

## Novelty classification

Classify the concept using evidence from current SOTA and market offerings:

1. Incremental
- Similar approach already deployed broadly.
- Differentiation is mostly UX, packaging, or minor cost/performance gains.

2. Adjacent
- Known methods adapted to a new segment, workflow, or distribution model.
- Differentiation depends on execution and go-to-market edge.

3. Distinct
- Materially different mechanism, architecture, or business model.
- Requires stronger evidence because novelty claims are high risk.

## Evidence confidence scale

Rate each major claim:
- High: multiple primary, recent, independent sources agree.
- Medium: partial support or mixed source quality.
- Low: mostly inferred or weakly sourced.

## Competitor SWOT template

For each top competitor:
- Strengths: what they do better right now.
- Weaknesses: known gaps your concept may exploit.
- Opportunities: spaces where market shift favors your approach.
- Threats: factors that can invalidate your advantage.

## Transfer-learning extraction

For analogous deployments, extract:
- Implementation pattern used
- Preconditions for success
- Failure modes encountered
- Controls/mitigations used
- What can transfer to current idea (directly vs with adaptation)

## Decision rubric

Recommend one:
- Proceed: evidence supports differentiation and feasibility.
- Proceed with pivot: promising, but core assumptions must shift.
- Hold: evidence gaps too large for confident investment.
- Do not proceed: market/SOTA reality invalidates core thesis.
